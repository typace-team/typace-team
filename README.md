## The Illusion of "Double Reduction"

That said, one cannot deny that China's "Double Reduction" policy has been an absolute failure in its grassroots execution for our generation. It remains entirely superficial and formalistic. The essence of Chinese education is a war over social class and resources, and the policy has done nothing to change the scarcity of those resources. 

Under the guise of *"reducing burdens inside schools while actually increasing them,"* both children and parents are pushing themselves to the brink of exhaustion.

### 🧒 For Children: The Boredom Corral
While after-school care services have solved the pickup problem for parents, many schools simply "corral" children inside classrooms to do homework. Students leave early and return late, spending up to 10–12 hours a day at school. They have lost the freedom to run around or simply daydream. As a result, psychological issues like depression and school burnout are manifesting at increasingly younger ages.

### 👥 For Parents: From Visible Anxiety to Invisible Panic
School homework seemingly decreased, but to guarantee high test scores, teachers now assign tasks "subtly." They will say: 
* *"It is recommended that parents buy this workbook for their child..."*
* *"It is recommended that parents conduct dictation at home and sign off on it..."*

Schools have covertly shifted the pressure of academic screening onto families. 
* **The anxiety of the past was visible:** tutoring fees, homework volume, and ranking lists. 
* **The anxiety of today is invisible:** the surface looks calm, but you never know if someone else's child is learning middle school physics ahead of time with a master tutor in a basement. 

This information blind spot brings a "fear of falling behind"—not knowing what others are doing, and not knowing what you should do—pushing parents' mental states to the absolute limit.

## The Hyper-Premiumization of Underground Tutoring

The "Double Reduction" crushed large-scale tutoring corporations, but the demand never left. Consequently, tutoring has gone "underground" and become hyper-premiumized:

* **The costs are soaring:** The massive, affordable group classes of the past are gone, replaced by one-on-one private tutors, "crowdfunded" private pods, or live-in tutors costing hundreds or thousands of yuan per hour.
* **The era of "family background warfare" has arrived:** Previously, it was a battle of "who is willing to spend money." Now, it is a battle of parents' information asymmetry, networking resources, and even their personal ability to step in and tutor. 

Today, China has full-time, underground tutoring sessions that cost an eye-watering 30,000 RMB a day—roughly $4,431.58 USD (based on the exchange rate as of June 22, 2026, 14:14:13). (To put this in perspective for overseas readers, compare this single day's cost to your own monthly salary; what makes it even more striking is that many of these participating parents are by no means wealthy). This is not merely an educational absurdity; it is the ultimate satire of a society spiraling into collective hysteria.What this 30,000 RMB a day buys is not knowledge at all. Instead, it buys an "immunity pass" for parents—a placebo to temporarily suppress their burning anxiety. By covertly shifting the pressure of academic screening onto families, the system has hijacked what should have been a long-distance race for children, turning it into a black hole of mental exhaustion that empties the pockets of the middle class and drives two generations to madness.The crowning absurdity is that this astronomical financial investment bears absolutely no proportion to the actual educational outcome. Driven by a paralyzing "fear of falling behind" and the feeling that they "cannot afford to lose," countless families are selling off whatever they own to dump hundreds of thousands, or even millions, of yuan into "one-on-one master tutoring."Yet, after all this sacrifice, the child might only secure a spot at a low-tier vocational college or fail the exams entirely. The entire family falls into "educational impoverishment," while the child suffers a total mental breakdown—becoming, quite literally, a "botched, unfinished project" on the education assembly line.

Ordinary families cannot find good teachers and cannot afford private tutors; they can only watch helplessly as resourceful families make stealthy progress "underwater." The anxiety multiplies. **The battlefield has shifted from open competition to a covert cold war, leaving ordinary families at an even greater disadvantage.**

> **The fundamental issue is that the finish line hasn't moved an inch, so how could the process possibly be lightened?** 

This is the core contradiction. As long as the "academic vs. vocational track division" after the High School Entrance Examination (which remains a strict 5:5 or 6:4 ratio in many places) exists, as long as the single-plank bridge of the College Entrance Examination (Gaokao) for elite resources remains, and as long as discrimination based on "first degree" (undergraduate alma mater) persists in social recruitment, the educational "finish line" will never change.

* **Before:** Everyone openly signed up for classes and paid for institutional tutoring. It was exhausting, but the path was clear.
* **Now:** The screening criteria have not been lowered, but the visible ladders have been snatched away. Parents are left to build their own ladders from scratch. This sense of powerlessness and panic is far more intense than before.

## A Fractured Reality: The Absurdity of an "AI Model School"

Therefore, our lives feel incredibly fractured. Our school is considered one of the absolute best public middle schools in a certain district of Shanghai, yet our peers are almost exclusively bookworms. 

Under such circumstances:
* **Our sleep quality is restricted.**
* **Our code quality is restricted.**

Every single day, we sit in class studying *Morality and the Rule of Law*, only to witness the distortion, failure, and utter absurdity of grassroots policy execution in reality! 

### The Ultimate Irony
We were literally publicly denounced by our head teacher just for logging into **Tongyi Qianwen** (a domestic AI LLM) on a classroom computer. 

Only later did we find out that **our school is officially designated as a "Model School for AI Promotion" in this district!** 

Our teachers even genuinely believe that AI has absolutely nothing to do with hardware and is purely just a software app. This only further proves that our school has never done anything more than execute superficial formalism that wastes national fiscal funds!
